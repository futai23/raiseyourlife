## Ordering of three-card lows (strongest first)

1. **Three wheel cards with an ace** (A-2-4) — premium.
2. **Zero-gappers** (2-3-4, 3-4-5, 4-5-6, 5-6-7, 6-7-8) — low + straightness.
   Top three almost always playable; 5-6-7 and 6-7-8 are marginal multiway but
   strong heads-up.
3. **Three to a six with an ace** (A-3-6) — strong, especially two-suited.
4. **One-gappers** — see Tier 2.
5. **Three to a 7/8 with an ace** — decent; one-way if the ace dies.
6. **Two-gappers** — see Tier 3, gutshot only.
7. **Razz lows** (three low, no ace, no straightness) — mostly fold.

## Tier 1 — Almost Always Playable

Playable regardless of exposed cards / action, barring rare exceptions.

- **Rolled-up trips** — AAA strongest, but many rate **555 best** (blocks low
  straights, gets action from higher pairs). Goal on 3rd: just get money in.
  Equally strong short-handed or multiway.
- **AA** — low kicker preferred (leaves a low draw, disguised either way).
  Attack from 3rd for scoops. **High kicker loses value** (can't make low; if
  the ace is buried, high intent is obvious).
- **Three suited low cards** — value varies widely with how live the flush is.
  (3♣4♣)5♣ with live clubs ≫ (7♥3♥)8♥ with dead hearts (nearly one-way, can't
  fight multiway).
- **Three wheel cards with an ace** (A-2-4) — virtually always play; raise/call
  is close when someone has completed; high-ante favours attacking.
- **Zero-gappers, no ace** — 2-3-4 / 3-4-5 / 4-5-6 almost always; 5-6-7 / 6-7-8
  marginal multiway, strong heads-up, attackable vs wide-opening high-ante games.
- **Three to a six with an ace** (A-3-6) — almost always, even with dead aces,
  especially two-suited.

### Tier-1 equity anchors

- **(6♠7♦)8♠ is 50% vs KK**, and **50% vs a strong A-2 low** like (5♥2♣)A♣.
  Zero-gappers have real muscle.
- Reraising an ace, vs an opponent completing ~100%: **~55% equity**. Reraised
  by AA: still **42–44%**, and dropping the bring-in's three-card low improves
  the overall spot.

## Tier 2 — Often Playable (play ~75%+ of the time dealt)

- **One-gappers, no ace** — 2-3-5, 2-4-5, 3-4-6, 3-5-6, 4-5-7, 4-6-7, 5-6-8,
  5-7-8. Want the key straight-out card live. Rougher 4-6-7 / 5-6-8 especially
  need the key card unseen (low isn't strong, so you lean on high potential).
  **Heads-up, 5-6-8 types play surprisingly well** and can attack a possible
  steal on 3rd.
- **Three to a 7 or 8 with an ace** — relatively strong, prefer short-handed,
  attack. **Value drops hard multiway**; with dead aces, barely better than a
  Razz hand like 2-6-7. Average players over-attach and get stuck in the middle.
- **Kings & Queens** — good only when the boards/action make you the big high
  favourite AND the pot won't go multiway. Fold to a non-steal ace completion;
  fold QQ to a king completing. **KK is ~57% vs an ace-up with two random
  down cards** — too much to fold vs a straightforward player only. High-ante
  6-handed with only an ace + bring-in behind: do NOT fold KK.
- **Low pair with an ace** — with live pair cards, lots of value; fine even
  sandwiched between a good low draw and a (non-ace) high pair, because the
  ace over-card kicker lets you continue and realize equity.
- **Flush draws with two low cards** — flush outs must be very live multiway;
  Stud 8 wants to *limit* the field (unlike Stud High). High card up → represent
  a high pair; low card up → attack to knock out raggedy lows stealing half.

### Tier-2 equity anchors

- **(8♠6♥)5♣ is a 53% favourite over (2♠3♥)4♣**, and only a **49% underdog to
  (A♠3♥)4♣**. The ability to make a higher pair offsets the inferior low draw.
  *(This is the number to cite when the player says middle-pair-plus-low has
  high equity vs a bare three-card low — they're right, heads-up.)*
- A **pair of jacks is ~54% vs an ace raising 100%**; with an ace kicker or a
  dead ace it rises to **~57%** → reraise.

## Tier 3 — Sometimes Playable (steal / specific-situation only)

Playable only when the board favours you, you can get heads-up vs a weak range,
or as an ante steal. The zone where average players leak the most by over-playing.

- **Two-gappers, no ace** — 2-3-6, 2-4-6, 2-5-6, 3-4-7, 3-5-7, 3-6-7, 4-5-8,
  4-6-8, 4-7-8. Gutshot only. Three-to-a-six can call a single complete; a
  complete + reraise in front is much trickier. Unsuited 2-4-6 vs a five
  completing and an ace reraising → muck (calling ok with two live suits).
- **Low pair with a low (connected) kicker, no ace** — e.g. **(5♣6♦)6♣**: pair
  plus a connected straight-flush-ish kicker, relatively decent. **~55% equity
  vs (A♠2♠)4♥, ~44% vs KK.** Playability often comes down to how many sixes are
  exposed. *(This is the source anchor for the 226/446 low-pair discussion.)*
  Contrast **(4♣7♦)4♠** — a *disconnected* low pair — steal/defend only; small
  pair means a low hand can pair up to win the high, far less potential.
- **Middle pairs — JJ / TT / 99** — big gap below KK/QQ. Early position 99/TT
  tend to fold (nearly every card to act is a threat; multiway they get an
  over-card when a low bricks). Fold to a higher up-card completing. JJ ~54% vs
  an ace raising 100%; with ace kicker / dead ace → ~57%, reraise. Mostly an
  open-complete from mid/late position; disguised (J♣J♥)3♣ can take more risk.
- **Low pair with a high kicker, no ace** — marginal; playable if the kicker
  beats some pairs you'd run into. Folded around to (K♥6♦)6♣ with only a queen,
  jack, deuce behind = clear complete. Over-card kicker + hidden nature +
  implied odds make it ok if reraised by QQ/JJ.
- **Razz lows** — three low, no ace, no next-card straightness, e.g. (2♣6♦)7♠.
  Builds second-best lows. Never call a legitimate complete; pure steal or
  bring-in defence only.
- **Steal holdings** — Ace-Broadway-Low (A♣Q♦)6♦, Wheel-Broadway-Wheel
  (4♣K♣)5♦, three high cards (J♣Q♦)K♦, ace-as-up-card (6♣9♣)A♠. Ace-up: always
  consider a steal with reasonable hole cards; opponents tighten and rarely
  reraise.
